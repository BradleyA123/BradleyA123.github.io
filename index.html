Dr.Martin Luther King Jr.

The man who fought for the rights of his people and the joining of a nation

The Beginning of an Era

This is essentially where it all began for Dr.King and his part in the civil rights era

  • 1929 - Dr.Martin Luther King Jr. is born

  • 1944 - Graduates High School at 15 and attends Morehouse College a little after

  • 1948 - Receives BA in sociology from Morehouse

  • 1953 - Marries Coretta Scott King

  • 1955 - Dr.King leads boycott of segregated Montgomery buses and gains reputation

  • 1956 - The U.S. Supreme Court prompts Montgomery buses to be desegregated

The Civil Rights Movement

This is a list of the most important events during the civil right movement of Dr.King

  • 1957 - Dr. King helps found the Southern Christian Leadership Conference

  • 1963 - Dr.King is arrested during anti-segregation protests in Birmingham, which prompts him to write "Letter from Birmingham Jail"

  • 1963 - Dr.King delivers his iconic "I have a dream" speech during the march on washington

  • 1965 - Dr.King and the Southern Christian Leadership Conference march from selma to Montgomery for voting rights

  • 1965 - Congress passes Voting Rights Act of 1965, which eventually elminated literacy tests and other restrictions that were meant to keep black from voting,

The End of an Era

The events that took place near the end of Dr.King's life


  • 1966 - Dr.King opens SCLC office in chicago to organize protests against housing and employment discrimination

  • 1967 - Dr.King plans Poor People's Campaign, which was meant to spark a conversation about redistributing wealth to help eliminate black poverty

  • 1968 - Dr.King is assasinated, prompting violent riots to erupt in more than 100 cities
